# V10.13n Monitoring Checklist

**Purpose:** Quick decision framework for whether to keep `V10.13n` as-is, tune it, or move to `V10.13o`.

**Version under review:** `V10.13n`  
**Focus:** Exit quality after evidence-based threshold tuning  
**Review window:** first 1h, then 2–3h, then after 50+ new exits

---

## 1. Immediate Goals

`V10.13n` should improve the **exit mix**, not just inflate short-term headline metrics.

Primary goals:

- Increase `PARTIAL_TP_*` exits
- Increase `TRAIL_*` exits
- Reduce `TIMEOUT_*` share
- Keep win rate near target
- Avoid closing winners too early

---

## 2. What To Check

### A. Exit Mix
Watch the `V10.13g/V10.13m EXIT` line and audit section.

Desired direction:

- `micro > 0`
- `partial=(x,y,z)` not all zero
- `trail > 0`
- `scratch` present but not dominant
- `timeout` share falling over time

Healthy pattern example:

```text
TP=1 SL=3 micro=4 be=2 partial=(3,5,2) trail=6 scratch=7 stag=0 harvest=9 t_profit=4 t_flat=18 t_loss=5
```

Unhealthy pattern example:

```text
TP=0 SL=2 micro=9 be=0 partial=(0,0,0) trail=0 scratch=2 stag=0 harvest=0 t_profit=3 t_flat=31 t_loss=6
```

Interpretation:
- Too many `micro`
- No `partial`
- No `trail`
- Timeouts still dominate
- Winners may be cut too early

---

### B. Core Performance Metrics

Track these together, not in isolation:

- **Win rate**
- **Profit factor**
- **Expectancy**
- **Drawdown**
- **Last trade age**

Good operating band:

- Win rate: around `54%–56%`
- Profit factor: `> 1.5`
- Expectancy: non-negative, ideally positive
- Drawdown: stable, not sharply expanding
- Last trade age: should not drift into long idle without explanation

Important:  
A temporary profit factor spike right after deployment is **not enough** to confirm success.

---

### C. Per-Coin Quality

Check whether gains are broad or carried by only one or two symbols.

Priority review:

- `BTC`
- `ETH`
- `SOL`
- `ADA`
- `DOT`

Rules of thumb:

- `BTC/ETH` should remain stable or improve
- `SOL` should not degrade sharply after new exits
- `ADA` and `DOT` should not keep dragging expectancy
- If one pair is consistently poor, it may need pair-level penalty or cooldown tuning

---

### D. “Too Early Exit” Risk

This is the key concern behind `V10.13n`.

Warning signs:

- `micro` rises fast
- `partial` stays near zero
- `trail` stays near zero
- Profit factor initially jumps, then fades
- Win rate stable but expectancy stalls
- Audit shows frequent near-misses for higher profit branches

Interpretation:  
The bot is taking tiny wins but not letting winners develop.

---

## 3. Review Timeline

### After 1 Hour
Goal: confirm patch is active and non-broken.

Checklist:

- [ ] Bot is running normally
- [ ] No runtime faults
- [ ] Exit counters are moving
- [ ] `micro` or `scratch` changed from baseline
- [ ] No obvious surge in losses
- [ ] No syntax/import/live-path failures

Decision:
- If all good -> continue monitoring
- If broken -> hotfix immediately

---

### After 2–3 Hours
Goal: assess behavioral shift.

Checklist:

- [ ] `partial` exits appeared at least a few times
- [ ] `trail` exits appeared
- [ ] `timeout` share is lower than before
- [ ] Win rate still near target
- [ ] Profit factor not collapsing
- [ ] No evidence that micro exits dominate everything

Decision:
- If yes -> keep `V10.13n`
- If mixed -> prepare selective tuning
- If no -> likely need `V10.13o`

---

### After 50+ New Exits
Goal: make the real decision.

Checklist:

- [ ] Exit mix diversified
- [ ] Timeout no longer dominant
- [ ] Expectancy improved or stayed stable
- [ ] No strong sign of early-profit clipping
- [ ] No pair entered obvious death spiral
- [ ] Audit data supports the new thresholds

Decision:
- Pass -> freeze thresholds and continue
- Partial pass -> small targeted patch
- Fail -> design `V10.13o`

---

## 4. Decision Matrix

### Keep V10.13n unchanged
Choose this if:

- `partial` and `trail` both increase
- `timeout` meaningfully decreases
- win rate stays around target
- profit factor remains healthy
- expectancy is flat-to-positive
- no major pair degradation

---

### Prepare V10.13o — “Less Early Profit Clipping”
Choose this if:

- `micro` rises sharply
- `partial` stays weak
- `trail` stays weak
- audit shows near-misses for larger exits
- winners are being harvested too fast

Likely next actions:

- raise `MICRO_TP` slightly
- delay scratch slightly
- keep partial thresholds but give more room before first harvest
- make trailing more structure-aware instead of purely earlier

---

### Prepare V10.13o — “Timeout Still Dominant”
Choose this if:

- `timeout` is still too high
- `scratch` and `partial` remain low
- `trail` remains near zero
- audit shows branches failing by narrow margins

Likely next actions:

- widen scratch band a bit more
- lower partial activation slightly
- adjust trailing retrace/activation balance
- review branch order again

---

### Prepare Pair-Level Patch
Choose this if:

- one or two symbols keep producing negative expectancy
- overall system looks fine but specific pairs drag results

Likely next actions:

- pair-level EV penalty
- pair-level cooldown multiplier
- minimum sample gating by pair+regime
- confidence penalty for persistent losers

---

## 5. Concrete Thresholds For Go / No-Go

### Good outcome
- `partial > 0`
- `trail > 0`
- `timeout` lower than baseline
- win rate `>= 54%`
- PF `> 1.5`
- expectancy `>= 0`

### Warning outcome
- `micro` much higher but `partial/trail` still near zero
- PF unstable
- expectancy flat or drifting down
- one pair worsening noticeably

### Bad outcome
- no real exit diversification
- timeouts still dominate
- PF drops below 1
- expectancy more negative
- long trade drought or repeated safety events

---

## 6. Minimal Log Snapshot To Save

For each review point, save:

```text
1. EXIT line
2. EXIT_AUDIT line
3. Obchody / Winrate / Zisk / Profit Factor / Expectancy
4. VYSLEDKY PO MENACH
5. Last trade age
6. Any runtime fault / watchdog / anomaly message
```

This is enough to compare before vs after without reading full logs.

---

## 7. Recommended Operator Action

**Do not patch immediately again.**

Recommended process:

1. Review after **1 hour**
2. Main evaluation after **2–3 hours**
3. Final decision after **50+ new exits**

Only patch sooner if you see:

- runtime fault
- zero exits changing
- strong regression in PF/expectancy
- obvious premature winner clipping

---

## 8. Final Rule

`V10.13n` is successful only if it improves **how profits are harvested**, not just how often trades close.

The best signal of success is:

- more `partial`
- more `trail`
- fewer `timeout`
- stable win rate
- better expectancy

If that combination does not appear, move to the next patch deliberately, not emotionally.
